About Me

From Fine Arts to Artificial Intelligence — a journey of creative problem-solving.

The Journey

My path to AI research wasn't linear — it started with a paintbrush, not a keyboard. I earned my Bachelor of Fine Arts from Mississippi State University in 2011, spending years immersed in visual design, illustration, and the principles of communicating complex ideas through imagery.

That foundation in visual thinking and creative problem-solving became unexpectedly valuable when I transitioned into technology. What began as freelance web development evolved into a deep fascination with how humans interact with machines — and eventually, how machines can learn from humans.

I returned to Mississippi State to pursue computer science, earning my Master's degree in 2018 and my Ph.D. in 2023, both with a 4.0 GPA. My research focused on IDE-based learning analytics, programming metacognition, and using machine learning to understand how novice programmers think and learn.

Research & Industry

Along the way, I've had the privilege of working at some remarkable places:

  • Sandia National Laboratories — Developed neural network architectures for language generation and NLP applications
  • Air Force Research Laboratory — Built attention-based neural machine translation systems using deep learning
  • Mississippi State University — Led AI research in autonomous manufacturing, generative design, and digital twins

I've taught over 200 students in ECE Capstone Design, established partnerships between academia and industry, and published research on machine learning for programming education that earned a Best Presentation Award at ASEE.

Philosophy

I believe technology should empower people, not replace them. My work sits at the intersection of human-centered AI and resilient systems — building tools that augment human capabilities while remaining accessible, understandable, and under user control.

The solarpunk aesthetic of this site isn't just visual — it reflects a genuine commitment to:

  • Privacy-first design: Your data should stay yours
  • Offline capability: Technology that works without constant connectivity
  • Community empowerment: Teaching others to build, not just consume
  • Sustainable computing: Efficient, low-power solutions

Current Focus

Today, I'm focused on:

  • Building offline-first AI systems that run locally without cloud dependencies
  • Developing RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) pipelines for private document Q&A
  • Teaching workshops on prompt engineering, AI tools, and vibecoding
  • Creating tutorials for resilient, self-hosted technology
  • Exploring the intersection of LLMs and education

Beyond the Screen

When I'm not coding or researching, you might find me exploring off-grid technology, experimenting with mesh networks, or thinking about how communities can build resilient infrastructure that doesn't depend on big tech.

The name "Apollo" in my projects isn't accidental — it represents that same spirit of ambitious, collaborative problem-solving that put humans on the moon. We can build remarkable things when we work together.

✦ Fun Fact

My BFA background still influences my work — I believe good interfaces are a form of visual communication, and that the best technical documentation is designed, not just written.
